The rules this version commits to

The pattern above happened because (a) Claude kept introducing new objections in later turns that weren't applied evenly to earlier picks, and (b) Claude treated each round as fresh rather than cumulative. v3 fixes both by pre-committing.

  • Zero new ideas. v3 cannot introduce a single concept that wasn't already in v1 or v2.
  • Every objection is on the table now. The 11 rejection criteria below are the complete set. If a new objection emerges later, it must be one of these 11 or it's spurious.
  • Every criterion is applied to every surviving pick. No selective skepticism. If Pulse-point perfume oil was demoted for funded-comp saturation, Tinted lip oil must answer the same question (and the matrix shows it does).
  • Stability score is the lock. Concepts that pass 9+ of 11 criteria are locked. Concepts that pass 6-8 are "alive but risky." Concepts under 6 are dead. The matrix is the source of truth, not the next turn's narrative.
  • Pre-committed change conditions. The only ways a locked pick can be demoted are listed in the pre-commitment table at the bottom. Anything else is Claude moving the goalposts and Afreen can call it out.
